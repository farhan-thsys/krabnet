## Critical Integration Gaps

### Gap 1: SubscribeFrame Broadcast Never Fires (GRPC-03)

**Severity:** CRITICAL
**Files:** `src/grpc.rs:51,60,75,322`

`KrabnetServer` creates a `broadcast::Sender<FrameUpdate>` and `subscribe_frame()` subscribes clients. But `frame_tx.send(...)` is never called anywhere — not in `ingest_event`, not after engine evaluation, nowhere. Subscribers block forever with zero updates.

**Fix:** After `engine.ingest()` in `ingest_event()`, construct a `FrameUpdate` and call `self.frame_tx.send(update)`.

### Gap 2: Tier3Worker Completely Disconnected (TIER3-01..04)

**Severity:** CRITICAL
**Files:** `src/bin/krabnet-server.rs:55`, `src/engine.rs`

```rust
let (tier3_worker, _tier3_sender) = Tier3Worker::new(Box::new(mock_client));
```

The `_tier3_sender` is immediately dropped. Neither `engine.rs` nor `grpc.rs` hold a `Tier3Sender` or call `try_send()`. The worker thread exits immediately on channel disconnection. All four TIER3 requirements pass unit tests in isolation but are unwired from the live system.

**Fix:** Engine or KrabnetServer must hold the `Tier3Sender` and call `try_send(Tier2Result {...})` after frame evaluation completes.

### Gap 3: MCP Binary Missing Phase 11 Hardening

**Severity:** MAJOR
**Files:** `src/bin/krabnet-mcp.rs:18-20`

`krabnet-mcp` uses `Engine::new(1024)` — no compaction worker, no coalescer, no fanout limiter. Long-running MCP sessions will accumulate unbounded differential tuples.

**Fix:** Use `Engine::with_config(1024, Some(10_000), None, None)` at minimum for compaction protection.
